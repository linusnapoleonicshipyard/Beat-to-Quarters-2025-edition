## üéØ Overview

Beat to Quarters brings the Age of Sail to life with ship-specific mechanics that capture the unique characteristics of each vessel. Every ship is truly individual, just as they were in reality.

### What Makes This Special

- **Individualized Ships**: Every vessel has unique statistics based on tonnage, crew quality, nationality, gun caliber, and sail plan
- **Realistic Mechanics**: Hull damage, sail loss, crew casualties, fire, boarding actions, and surrender
- **Historical Accuracy**: Based on documented naval actions and period sources
- **Flexible Gameplay**: Solo, head-to-head, or fleet actions
- **Era Coverage**: Revolutionary War through Napoleonic Wars (1775-1815)

## üé≤ Game Mechanics

**Scale**: 1 turn = 1 minute | 1mm = 1 meter  
**Required**: Percentage dice, D6, measuring tools, miniature ships, Battle Log sheets

### Key Systems

- **Movement**: Point of sail system with realistic turning restrictions
- **Gunnery**: Detailed firing mechanics with range, angle, and nationality modifiers
- **Damage**: Hull value tracking, gun loss, mast damage, crew casualties
- **Morale**: Surrender points based on damage and crew losses
- **Boarding**: Multi-stage boarding combat system
- **Fire**: Dangerous fire progression and damage mechanics

## üÜï 2025 Edition

This edition represents a collaboration between original designer Barry J. Fox and Linus Spjutsberg, who discovered the rules in 2025.

### What's New

**Designer Updates** (Barry J. Fox):
- Revised nationality modifiers based on sources published after 1981
- Updated Danish and Swedish statistics
- Minor corrections and clarifications

**2025 Additions** (Linus Spjutsberg):
- Complete transcription and professional formatting
- House rules developed through playtesting:
  - Enhanced fire mechanics
  - Detailed gun crew casualties
  - Speed reduction from sail loss
  - Magazine flooding effects
- Digital companion app for automated calculations
- Redesigned play aids and gauges
- Modern document layout
